A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the “Missile Man of India,” was an esteemed Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble background and went on to study physics and aerospace engineering. Kalam played a pivotal role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname “Missile Man.” He was also a key figure in India’s nuclear weapons tests in 1998. Beyond his scientific achievements, Kalam was a beloved teacher and author, inspiring millions with his vision for India’s future and his emphasis on education and youth empowerment.
